Such activities can give a temporary boost to your well-being, but often this boost is short lasting. This means that one is required to engage in such activities again and again in order to feel good.
Although there is nothing abnormal in itself about this, it could be problematic in a few situations:
a) When these are often carried out at the cost of/in ways that interfere with your doing other activities that do not give you immediate pleasure but are useful for you in the longer run,
b) When these pleasure-oriented activities are harmful for your health
c) Sometimes, frequently engaging in such activities can actually be a marker of feeling distressed. There are times when we try to cope with our negative feelings (e.g. sadness, hurt, anxiety or boredom) by seeking out pleasure –oriented activities to get a quick relief/to feel better. This can lift our mood help us to an extent, but we need to simultaneously figure out how to deal with the underlying distress and think of other methods of coping too which have longer term benefits.
Therefore we need to balance these with meaning-oriented activities that can enhance our overall well-being.
